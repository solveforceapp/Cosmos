# Band

## Definition

A band is a specific range of frequencies within the electromagnetic spectrum allocated for a particular communication purpose. Bands are used in radio, television, cellular networks, Wi-Fi, satellite, and many other technologies to organize and manage signal transmission and reception.

## How It Works

Regulatory bodies assign frequency bands to different services to prevent interference and ensure efficient use of the spectrum. Devices are designed to operate within designated bands, using modulation and filtering techniques to transmit and receive signals. Bandwidth, or the width of a band, determines how much data can be carried. Bands can be narrow (for specialized uses) or wide (for high-capacity services).

## Real-World Application

Bands are critical for wireless communication, broadcasting, navigation, and scientific research. Mobile phones use multiple bands to connect to networks worldwide. TV and radio stations broadcast on assigned bands. Wi-Fi routers operate in the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, supporting different speeds and ranges.
