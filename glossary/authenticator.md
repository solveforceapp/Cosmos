# Authenticator

## Definition

An authenticator is a tool, device, or process used to verify the identity of a user, device, or system. Authenticators provide the evidence required for authentication, ensuring that only legitimate parties gain access to protected resources.

## How It Works

Authenticators can be something you know (password), something you have (security token, smart card), or something you are (biometric data). Software authenticators generate one-time codes, while hardware authenticators may use cryptographic keys. Multi-factor authenticators combine two or more methods for enhanced security.

## Real-World Application

Authenticators are used in online banking, corporate networks, and personal devices. They protect accounts, enable secure transactions, and support regulatory compliance. Popular examples include Google Authenticator, YubiKey, and fingerprint scanners.
