# Balun

## Definition

A balun (short for "balanced to unbalanced") is an electronic device that connects different types of transmission lines, such as coaxial cables (unbalanced) and twisted pair wires (balanced). Baluns enable compatibility, minimize signal loss, and reduce interference in network cabling and radio frequency (RF) systems.

## How It Works

Baluns convert signals from balanced to unbalanced formats (and vice versa), matching impedance and maintaining signal integrity. They are used in audio, video, networking, and antenna systems. By isolating and transforming signals, baluns prevent ground loops, crosstalk, and electromagnetic interference.

## Real-World Application

Baluns are found in Ethernet networks, CCTV installations, broadcast studios, and ham radio setups. They enable long-distance transmission, support high-quality audio/video, and simplify integration of diverse equipment.
