# GPON (Gigabit Passive Optical Network)

## Definition

GPON (Gigabit Passive Optical Network) is a fiber-optic broadband technology that uses passive splitters to deliver high-speed internet to multiple endpoints over a single optical fiber. GPON is a key enabler of modern fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) and fiber-to-the-building (FTTB) deployments.

## How It Works

GPON networks use a point-to-multipoint architecture, where a single fiber from the providerâ€™s central office is split to serve many customers. Each user receives dedicated bandwidth, and the system supports gigabit speeds for both uploads and downloads. GPON is managed for security, quality of service, and scalability.

## Real-World Application

ISPs and telecoms use GPON to deliver broadband, TV, and voice services to homes, businesses, and campuses. GPON supports smart cities, IoT, and cloud applications.

Imagine a single express train that branches off to deliver passengers to every neighborhood, all traveling at lightning speed. Or picture a river that splits into many streams, each bringing fresh water to a different village.

## Why It Matters

GPON brings affordable, high-speed connectivity to communities, driving digital transformation and economic growth.
