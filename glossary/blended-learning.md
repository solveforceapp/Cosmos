# Blended Learning

## Definition

Blended learning is an educational approach that combines traditional face-to-face instruction with online or digital learning experiences. This hybrid model leverages the strengths of both environments, offering flexibility, personalization, and enhanced engagement for learners.

## How It Works

In blended learning, students may attend in-person classes while also accessing online resources, assignments, and assessments. Technology enables self-paced study, interactive content, and collaboration beyond the classroom. Teachers can tailor instruction to individual needs, track progress, and provide timely feedback.

## Real-World Application

Blended learning is used in schools, universities, corporate training, and professional development. It supports remote learning, flipped classrooms, and lifelong education. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the adoption of blended models worldwide.

## Why It Matters

Blended learning empowers learners and educators, making education more accessible, effective, and adaptable to changing needs.
