# Bundle

## Definition

A bundle is a packaged deal that includes multiple services, such as call minutes, internet, television, or mobile data, offered together at a single price. Bundles are common in telecommunications, media, and technology industries, providing convenience, value, and flexibility for consumers and businesses.

## How It Works

Service providers create bundles by combining related or complementary services into a single contract or subscription. Customers pay one fee for the entire package, often receiving discounts or added features compared to purchasing each service separately. Bundles can be customized to meet specific needs, such as family plans, business packages, or entertainment bundles.

## Real-World Application

Bundles are used by telecom companies to offer phone, internet, and TV services together (triple play), or by cloud providers to combine storage, security, and productivity tools. Bundles simplify billing, enhance customer loyalty, and encourage the adoption of new services. Regulatory bodies may define rules for bundling to ensure fair competition and consumer protection.
