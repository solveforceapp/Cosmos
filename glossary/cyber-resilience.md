# Cyber Resilience

## Definition

Cyber resilience is the ability of an organization to prepare for, respond to, and recover from cyberattacks or disruptions while maintaining essential operations and minimizing impact. It combines cybersecurity, business continuity, and risk management.

## How It Works

Cyber resilience involves proactive planning, incident response, backup systems, and regular testing. Organizations assess risks, implement controls, and train staff to adapt to evolving threats.

## Real-World Application

Cyber resilience is critical for critical infrastructure, healthcare, and financial services. It ensures organizations can withstand ransomware, outages, and supply chain attacks.

Imagine a ship that can weather any storm, quickly repairing damage and staying on course.

## Entertaining Example

Picture a superhero team that bounces back from every setback, always ready for the next challenge.

## Why It Matters

Cyber resilience is the foundation of trust and stability in the digital era.
