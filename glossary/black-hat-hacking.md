# Black Hat Hacking

## Definition

Black hat hacking refers to the practice of breaking into computer systems, networks, or applications for malicious purposes. Black hat hackers exploit vulnerabilities to steal data, disrupt operations, or gain unauthorized access, often for personal gain or to cause harm. This contrasts with ethical (white hat) hacking, which aims to improve security.

## How It Works

Black hat hackers use a variety of techniques, including malware, phishing, social engineering, and zero-day exploits. They may target individuals, businesses, or governments, seeking financial rewards, sensitive information, or notoriety. Law enforcement and cybersecurity professionals work to detect, prevent, and prosecute black hat activities.

## Real-World Application

Black hat hacking is responsible for many high-profile data breaches, ransomware attacks, and cybercrimes. Organizations invest in security tools, training, and ethical hacking to defend against these threats. Public awareness and strong policies are essential for reducing risk.
