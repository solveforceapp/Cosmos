# Neurogenesis

Neurogenesis is the process by which new neurons are generated from neural stem or progenitor cells. While most neurogenesis occurs during embryonic development, it also takes place in certain regions of the adult brain, such as the hippocampus. Neurogenesis is fundamental to brain development, learning, memory, and recovery from injury.

## Mechanism

Neural stem cells divide and differentiate into neurons, glia, and other cell types. The process is regulated by genetic, molecular, and environmental factors, including growth factors, hormones, and neural activity.

## Applications in Biotechnology

- **Brain Repair:** Stimulating neurogenesis is explored as a strategy for treating brain injuries, stroke, and neurodegenerative diseases.
- **Drug Development:** Compounds that enhance or inhibit neurogenesis are screened for potential therapies for depression, cognitive decline, and epilepsy.
- **Stem Cell Research:** Understanding neurogenesis guides the development of cell-based therapies for neurological disorders.

## Applications in Neurotechnology

- **Brain Imaging:** Techniques to visualize neurogenesis help study brain plasticity and disease progression.
- **Cognitive Enhancement:** Research explores ways to boost neurogenesis for improving learning and memory.
