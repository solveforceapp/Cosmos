# Asynchronous Transfer Mode (ATM)

## Definition

Asynchronous Transfer Mode (ATM) is a high-speed networking technology that transmits data in fixed-size cells (53 bytes each) at rates up to several gigabits per second. ATM supports the integration of voice, video, and data on a single network, making it a versatile solution for broadband communication.

## How It Works

ATM divides information into small, uniform cells, which are routed independently across the network. This approach enables efficient, predictable delivery of different types of traffic, including real-time voice and video. ATM’s quality of service (QoS) features allow for prioritization and guaranteed bandwidth.

## Real-World Application

ATM was widely used in telecommunications, enterprise networks, and internet backbones during the 1990s and early 2000s. It enabled the convergence of multiple services on a single infrastructure, paving the way for modern broadband and multimedia networks.

## Why It Matters

ATM set the stage for today’s integrated, high-speed networks, demonstrating the power of standardization and efficiency in communication.
