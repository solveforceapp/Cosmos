# Change Management

## Definition

Change management is a structured approach to planning, implementing, and monitoring changes within an organization. It aims to minimize disruptions, manage risks, and ensure smooth transitions in IT systems, business processes, and organizational culture.

## How It Works

Change management involves assessing the need for change, developing a plan, communicating with stakeholders, and providing training and support. Changes are documented, tested, and reviewed before implementation. Feedback and continuous improvement are key components.

## Real-World Application

Change management is used in software development, mergers, process improvements, and digital transformation. It helps organizations adapt to new technologies, regulations, and market conditions. Successful change management increases adoption, reduces resistance, and delivers better outcomes.
