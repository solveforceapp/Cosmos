# Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

## Definition

A Business Continuity Plan (BCP) is a comprehensive set of procedures and strategies designed to ensure that critical business functions can continue or be quickly restored during and after a disruption. BCPs address risks such as natural disasters, cyberattacks, equipment failures, and pandemics.

## How It Works

BCPs identify essential operations, assign responsibilities, and outline steps for recovery. Plans include backup systems, alternative work locations, communication protocols, and regular testing. BCPs are living documents, updated as risks and business needs evolve.

## Real-World Application

Organizations of all sizes use BCPs to minimize downtime, protect assets, and maintain customer trust. Regulatory requirements often mandate BCPs in finance, healthcare, and critical infrastructure. The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the importance of robust continuity planning.
