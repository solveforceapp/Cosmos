# Bandwidth

## Definition

Bandwidth is the maximum rate at which data can be transmitted over a communication channel, measured in bits per second (bps) or its multiples (kbps, Mbps, Gbps). Bandwidth determines how much information can flow through a network, affecting speed, capacity, and user experience.

## How It Works

Bandwidth is influenced by the physical medium (fiber, copper, wireless), network equipment, and signal quality. Higher bandwidth allows for faster downloads, smoother streaming, and more simultaneous connections. Bandwidth can be shared (as in broadband) or dedicated (as in leased lines).

## Real-World Application

Bandwidth is a key metric for internet service providers, businesses, and consumers. It impacts everything from video calls and online gaming to cloud computing and IoT devices. Managing bandwidth ensures critical applications get the resources they need.

Imagine a water pipe: the wider the pipe, the more water (data) can flow at once.

Picture a highway where adding more lanes lets more cars travel side by side, reducing traffic jams and speeding up the journey.

## Why It Matters

Bandwidth is the lifeblood of digital communication, shaping the performance and possibilities of our connected world.
