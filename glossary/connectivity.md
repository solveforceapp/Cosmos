# Connectivity

## Definition

Connectivity is the ability of devices, systems, or networks to establish and maintain communication and data exchange. It is essential for digital operations, services, and the Internet of Things (IoT).

## How It Works

Connectivity relies on physical media (cables, wireless signals), protocols, and network infrastructure. It can be local (Bluetooth), wide-area (cellular), or global (internet).

## Real-World Application

Connectivity powers smart homes, autonomous vehicles, and global commerce. It enables real-time data sharing, remote monitoring, and seamless user experiences.

Imagine a web of invisible threads linking every device, person, and place.

## Entertaining Example

Picture a digital handshake that instantly connects you to friends, services, and information worldwide.

## Why It Matters

Connectivity is the lifeblood of the digital age, driving innovation and opportunity everywhere.
