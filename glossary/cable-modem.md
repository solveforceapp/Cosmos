# Cable Modem

## Definition

A cable modem is a device that connects a computer or network to the internet using cable television (CATV) lines. Cable modems provide high-speed broadband access, supporting fast downloads, streaming, and online gaming for homes and businesses.

## How It Works

Cable modems modulate and demodulate data signals over coaxial cables, sharing bandwidth with television services. They connect to a service providerâ€™s headend, which routes internet traffic to and from the user. Modern cable modems support DOCSIS standards for higher speeds and reliability.

## Real-World Application

Cable modems are widely used in urban and suburban areas, offering affordable, always-on internet access. They support multiple devices, Wi-Fi routers, and smart home systems. Service quality depends on network congestion and infrastructure.
