# Call Forwarding

## Definition

Call forwarding is a telecommunications feature that automatically redirects incoming calls to another phone number, extension, or voicemail. Call forwarding ensures that important calls are answered, even when the original recipient is unavailable or out of the office.

## How It Works

Users activate call forwarding through phone settings, service codes, or PBX systems. Calls can be forwarded unconditionally, when busy, or after a set number of rings. Advanced systems allow for time-based or selective forwarding.

## Real-World Application

Call forwarding is essential for businesses, remote workers, and customer service teams. It supports mobility, continuity, and responsiveness. Call centers use forwarding to balance workloads and improve customer satisfaction.

Imagine a personal assistant who always knows where you are and makes sure your calls reach you, no matter what.
