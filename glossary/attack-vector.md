# Attack Vector

## Definition

An attack vector is a path or method used by cybercriminals to gain unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, or data. Attack vectors exploit vulnerabilities, weaknesses, or user behavior to deliver malware, steal information, or disrupt operations. Understanding attack vectors is crucial for building effective defenses.

## How It Works

Attack vectors can be technical (exploiting software bugs), social (phishing, social engineering), or physical (unauthorized device access). Common vectors include email attachments, malicious websites, unsecured Wi-Fi, and insider threats. Attackers often use multiple vectors in combination to increase their chances of success.

## Real-World Application

Organizations analyze attack vectors to identify risks, implement security controls, and train employees. Security tools like firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and endpoint protection help block or mitigate attack vectors. Regular updates and awareness programs are essential for staying ahead of evolving threats.
