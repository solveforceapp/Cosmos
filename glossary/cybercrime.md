# Cybercrime

## Definition

Cybercrime refers to criminal activities carried out using computers, networks, or the internet. Examples include hacking, identity theft, fraud, and the distribution of malware.

## How It Works

Cybercriminals exploit vulnerabilities, use phishing, or deploy malicious software to steal data, extort money, or disrupt services. Law enforcement agencies investigate and prosecute cybercrimes, often across international borders.

## Real-World Application

Cybercrime affects individuals, businesses, and governments. Victims may suffer financial loss, reputational damage, or privacy violations. Cybersecurity awareness and best practices help reduce risk.

## Why It Matters

Cybercrime is a growing threat, requiring vigilance, education, and cooperation to combat.
