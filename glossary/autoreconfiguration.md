# Autoreconfiguration

## Definition

Autoreconfiguration is a self-healing process in computer networks, particularly Token Ring networks, that automatically detects, diagnoses, and reconfigures around failures or disruptions. This capability helps maintain network reliability, minimize downtime, and ensure continuous operation.

## How It Works

When a fault is detected—such as a broken cable or malfunctioning node—the network initiates autoreconfiguration. It isolates the problem area, reroutes traffic, and restores connectivity without manual intervention. Diagnostic tools and protocols support this process, enabling rapid recovery.

## Real-World Application

Autoreconfiguration is used in enterprise networks, industrial automation, and mission-critical systems. It supports high availability, disaster recovery, and business continuity. Modern networks extend autoreconfiguration concepts to wireless, mesh, and software-defined environments.

Autoreconfiguration enhances resilience, reduces maintenance costs, and supports the reliability demanded by today’s digital infrastructure.
