# Data Breach

## Definition

A data breach is an incident where sensitive, confidential, or protected data is accessed, disclosed, or stolen by unauthorized individuals or systems. Data breaches can have serious financial, legal, and reputational consequences.

## How It Works

Breaches occur through hacking, phishing, malware, or insider threats. Organizations must detect, contain, and report breaches, and notify affected parties as required by law.

## Real-World Application

Data breaches affect businesses, governments, and individuals. High-profile cases include credit card leaks, healthcare data theft, and government espionage.

Imagine a thief breaking into a vault and stealing secret documents, leaving chaos in their wake.

## Entertaining Example

Picture a digital cat burglar sneaking through firewalls to snatch valuable data jewels.

## Why It Matters

Data breaches highlight the importance of strong security and vigilance in the digital age.
