# Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP)

Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is the primary energy carrier in all living cells, often referred to as the "molecular currency" of biological energy. In biotechnology and neurotechnology, ATP is central to understanding cellular metabolism, neural signaling, and the development of biosensors and medical diagnostics.

## Structure and Function

ATP consists of an adenosine molecule bonded to three phosphate groups. When a phosphate bond is broken (hydrolyzed), energy is released to power cellular processes such as muscle contraction, nerve impulse transmission, and biosynthesis.

## Applications in Biotechnology

- **Cellular Metabolism:** ATP levels are measured to assess cell viability, metabolic activity, and the effects of drugs or toxins.
- **Biosensors:** ATP-detecting biosensors are used in food safety, environmental monitoring, and medical diagnostics.
- **Biomanufacturing:** ATP is monitored in fermentation and cell culture processes to optimize yields and product quality.

## Applications in Neurotechnology

- **Neural Activity:** ATP is released during synaptic transmission and is involved in signaling between neurons and glial cells.
- **Brain Imaging:** Techniques that track ATP dynamics help researchers study brain metabolism and neurodegenerative diseases.
