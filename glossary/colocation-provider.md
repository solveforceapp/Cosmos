# Colocation Provider

## Definition

A colocation provider is a company that operates data centers and offers colocation services, providing secure, reliable, and connected environments for customers’ IT equipment. They ensure uptime, connectivity, and compliance for mission-critical systems.

## How It Works

Colocation providers offer space, power, cooling, and network access. Customers install their own servers and devices, while the provider manages the facility, security, and support services. Providers often offer additional services like remote hands, monitoring, and compliance assistance.

## Real-World Application

Colocation providers serve enterprises, cloud companies, and government agencies. They enable hybrid cloud, disaster recovery, and global expansion strategies.
