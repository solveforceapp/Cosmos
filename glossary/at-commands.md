# AT Commands

## Definition

AT commands (short for Attention commands) are a set of instructions used to control modems and other communication devices. Each command begins with the letters "AT," signaling the device to pay attention and execute the specified action. AT commands enable dialing, configuration, diagnostics, and data transfer in telecommunication systems.

## How It Works

Users or software send AT commands to a modem via a serial interface. The modem interprets the command and responds accordingly—dialing a number, answering a call, or changing settings. AT commands are standardized, making them compatible across different devices and manufacturers.

## Real-World Application

AT commands are used in dial-up modems, mobile phones, IoT devices, and industrial equipment. They enable automation, remote management, and troubleshooting. Developers and network engineers rely on AT commands to test connectivity, configure networks, and integrate devices.

Imagine a remote control for your modem, allowing you to issue precise instructions from anywhere.

AT commands are the universal language of modem control, enabling flexible, programmable communication in countless applications.
